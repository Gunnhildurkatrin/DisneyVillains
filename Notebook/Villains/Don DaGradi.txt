"{{Infobox Real Person\n|image = don-da-gradi-feat.jpg|thumb|250px\n|born = [[September 29]], [[Pre-1922#1911|1911]]<br>[[New York City]], [[New York]], [[United States]]\n|died = [[August 4]], [[1991]] (aged 79)<br>Friday Harbor, [[Washington]], United States\n|nationality = [[File:Flag of the United States.png|20px]] American\n|occupation(s) = Screenwriter, layout artist\n|years active = [[1941]]-[[1982]]}}'''Don DaGradi''' was an American screenwriter and layout artist, best known for his work with [[The Walt Disney Company|Disney]]. DaGradi started out as a layout artist on 1940s cartoons, such as ''[[Der Fuehrer's Face]]''. He also worked as a color and styling or sequence consultant on many other Disney films. DaGradi is probably best known for his work on ''[[Mary Poppins (film)|Mary Poppins]]'', for which he earned an Oscar nomination for Best Original Screenplay alongside Bill Walsh. He was posthumously named a [[Disney Legends|Disney Legend]] in [[1991]], only months after his death.\n\nIn the [[2013]] film, ''[[Saving Mr. Banks]]'', DaGradi was portrayed by [[Bradley Whitford]].\n\n==Disney filmography==\n===Feature films===\n*''[[Dumbo (1941 film)|Dumbo]]'' ([[1941]]) - Art Director\n*''[[Victory Through Air Power]]'' ([[1943]]) - Art Director\n*''[[The Three Caballeros]]'' ([[1944]]) - Layout artist\n*''[[Make Mine Music]]'' ([[1946]]) - Layout artist\n*''[[Fun and Fancy Free]]'' ([[1947]]) - Layout artist\n*''[[The Adventures of Ichabod and Mr. Toad]]'' ([[1949]]) - color and styling\n*''[[Cinderella (1950 film)|Cinderella]]'' ([[1950]]) - color and styling\n*''[[Alice in Wonderland (1951 film)|Alice in Wonderland]]]]'' ([[1951]]) - color and styling\n*''[[Peter Pan (film)|Peter Pan]]'' ([[1953]]) - color and styling\n*''[[Lady and the Tramp]]'' ([[1955]]) - story\n*''[[Sleeping Beauty]]'' ([[1959]]) - production design\n*''[[Darby O'Gill and the Little People]]'' ([[1959]]) - special art styling\n*''[[Kidnapped]]'' ([[1960]]) - story sketches\n*''[[Pollyanna]]'' (1960) - sequence consultant\n*''[[The Absent-Minded Professor]]'' ([[1961]]) - sequence consultant\n*''[[The Parent Trap]]'' (1961) - sequence consultant\n*''[[Son of Flubber]]'' ([[1963]]) - screenplay\n*''[[Mary Poppins (film)|Mary Poppins]]'' ([[1964]]) - screenplay\n*''[[Lt.
