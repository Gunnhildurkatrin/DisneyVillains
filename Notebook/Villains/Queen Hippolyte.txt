She is the monarchical leader to the Amazons, a tribe of all-female exclusive warriors, famous in Greece and is the mother of [[Tempest]].\n\n==Role in the [[Hercules (TV series)|series]]==\nIn \"[[Hercules and the Girdle of Hippolyte]]\", while training with Phil, Hercules learns that during a Damsel-in-Distress training he was rescuing a guy doll. Phil tells him that heroes shouldn't save men they only save damsels. Meanwhile after Queen Hippolyte believes that her daughter, [[Tempest]] is going soft and weak, she kidnaps her, and makes her undergo a savage initiation ceremony to regain her status as an Amazonian warrior and to earn the right to wear the Golden Girdle. Hercules follows in a misguided rescue attempt only to learn she's just fine.\n\n==Trivia==\n*In the original myth, Queen Hippolyte was Ares' daughter and Hercules' ninth labor.\n*She is only mentioned in the movie, ''[[Hercules (film)|Hercules]]''.\n*Queen Hippolyte makes a cameo appearance in the ''[[Once Upon a Time]]'' television series, represented as one of Hercules' twelve medals.\n\n{{Hercules}}\n{{DEFAULTSORT:Hippolyte, Queen}}\n[[Category:Queens]]\n[[Category:Females]]\n[[Category:Warriors]]\n[[Category:Greek Mythology]]\n[[Category:Greek characters]]\n[[Category:Hercules characters]]\n[[Category:Once Upon a Time characters]]\n[[Category:Neutral characters]]\n[[Category:Royalty]]\n[[Category:Parents]]\n[[Category:Military characters]]\n[[Category:Disney characters]]\n[[Category:Animated characters]]\n[[Category:TV Animation characters]]"
