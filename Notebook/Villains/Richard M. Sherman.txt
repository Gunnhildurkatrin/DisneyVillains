"{{Infobox Real Person\n|image = Richard-m-sherman-137811l.jpg\n|name = Richard M. Sherman\n|born = Richard Morton Sherman<br>[[June 12]], [[1928]] (age 93) <br>[[New York City]], [[New York]], [[United States]]\n|nationality = [[File:Flag of the United States.png|20px]] American\n|occupation(s) = Composer, lyricist, screenwriter, publisher, songwriter\n|years active = [[1950]]-present\n|spouse = Joan Newman ([[1949]]-?; divorced)<br>Elizabeth Gluck ([[1957]]-present)\n|children = Lynda (with Joan)<br>Gregory and Victoria (with Elizabeth)\n}}'''Richard Morton Sherman''' is an American composer, lyricist, screenwriter, publisher, and songwriter, who specialized in writing for musical films with his brother, [[Robert B. Sherman]], known as the [[Sherman Brothers]].\n\nSome of the Sherman Brothers' best-known writing includes the songs from ''[[Mary Poppins]]'', ''[[The Jungle Book]]'', the ''[[Winnie the Pooh (franchise)|Winnie the Pooh]]'' franchise, ''{{WikipediaLink|Chitty Chitty Bang Bang}}'', and the Disney theme park songs \"[[It's a Small World (song)|It's a Small World]]\" and \"[[There's a Great Big Beautiful Tomorrow]]\".\n\n==Early life==\nRichard Morton Sherman was born in [[New York City]] to Russian-Jewish immigrants, Rosa and {{WikipediaLink|Al Sherman}}. Together with his older brother Robert, [[Sherman Brothers|The Sherman Brothers]] eventually followed in their songwriting father's footsteps to form a long-lasting songwriting partnership.\n\nFollowing seven years of frequent cross-country moves, the Sherman family finally settled down in [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beverly_Hills,_California Beverly Hills, California] in [[1937]]. Throughout Richard's years at [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beverly_Hills_High_School Beverly Hills High School] he became fascinated with music and studied several instruments including the flute, piccolo, and piano.\n\nAt his [[1946]] graduation from Beverly Hills High School, Richard Sherman and [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andr\u00e9_Previn Andr\u00e9 Previn] played a musical duet. Previn played piano and Sherman played flute. Coincidentally, in [[1965]] both composers (along with Richard's brother, [[Robert B. Sherman|Robert]]) won [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academy_Awards Oscars] in music categories for different films.\n\nAt Bard College, Sherman majored in Music, writing numerous sonatas and \"art songs.\" His ambition to write the \"Great American Symphony\" eventually led him to write songs. Within two years of graduating, Richard and Robert Sherman began writing songs together on a challenge from their father, songwriter Al Sherman.\n\n==Songwriting career==\nIn [[1965]], the Sherman Brothers won 2 Academy Awards for ''[[Mary Poppins (film)|Mary Poppins]]'', including \"[[Feed the Birds]]\", \"[[Supercalifragilisticexpialidocious]]\", and the Oscar winner, \"[[Chim Chim Cher-ee]]\". Since ''Mary Poppins''<nowiki/>' premiere, Sherman has subsequently earned 9 Academy Award nominations, 2 Grammy Awards, 4 Grammy Award nominations and 23 gold and platinum albums.\n\nRobert and Richard Sherman worked directly for [[Walt Disney]] until Disney's death in [[1966]]. Since leaving the company, the brothers worked freelance as songwriters on scores of motion pictures, television shows, theme park exhibits and stage musicals.\n\nTheir first non-Disney assignment came with {{WikipediaLink|Albert R. Broccoli}}'s motion picture production ''{{WikipediaLink|Chitty Chitty Bang Bang}}'' in [[1968]] which garnered the brothers their third Academy Award Nomination. In [[1973]], the Sherman Brothers made history by becoming the only Americans ever to win First Prize at the Moscow Film Festival for ''[[wikipedia:Tom Sawyer (1973 film)|Tom Sawyer]]'' for which they also authored the screenplay.\n\n''{{WikipediaLink|The Slipper and the Rose}}'' was picked to be the Royal Command Performance of the year and was attended by Her Majesty, [[wikipedia:Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother|Queen Elizabeth, the Queen Mother]]. A modern musical adaptation of the classic ''Cinderella'' story, ''Slipper'' also features both song-score and screenplay by the Sherman Brothers. That same year the Sherman Brothers received their star on the [[Hollywood]] \"Walk of Fame\" directly across from Grauman's Chinese Theater.\n\n==Later career==\nIn [[2000]], the Sherman Brothers wrote songs for Disney's blockbuster film, ''[[The Tigger Movie]]'' (2000). This film marked the brothers' first major motion picture for [[the Walt Disney Company]] in over 28 years.\n\nIn 2003, four Sherman Brothers' musicals ranked in the \"Top 10 Favorite Children's Films of All Time\" in a ([[United Kingdom|British]]) nationwide poll reported by the BBC. ''[[The Jungle Book]]'' ([[1967]]) ranked at #7, ''Mary Poppins'' (1964) ranked at #8 and ''[[The Aristocats]]'' ([[1970]]) ranked at #9.\n\nDisney and [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameron_Mackintosh Cameron Mackintosh]'s musical production of ''[[Mary Poppins (musical)|Mary Poppins]]'' made its world premiere at the Prince Edward Theatre in December [[2004]] and features the Sherman Brothers' classic songs.\n\nDuring a [[London]] press junket promoting the 40th anniversary DVD re-release of ''The Jungle Book'', Robert and Richard Sherman were witnessed by press working on a new song for Inkas in the same Brown's Hotel room, where ''The Jungle Book'' was originally penned by British writer, [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudyard_Kipling Rudyard Kipling], over a hundred years earlier.\n\nIn May [[2009]], a documentary called ''[[The Boys: The Sherman Brothers' Story]]'' was released. In October 2009, Disney released a 59 track, two CD compendium of their work for the studio spanning forty-two years. The CD is entitled: \"The Sherman Brothers Songbook\".\n\nOn [[March 11]], [[2010]], the Sherman Brothers were presented with a Window on [[Main Street, U.S.A. (Disneyland)|Main Street]] [[Disneyland]] in [[Anaheim]], [[California]] in honor of their contribution to Disney theme parks. On [[May 17]], 2010 the \"Career Achievement Award\" at The Theatre Museum's 2010 Awards Gala.\n\nOn January 10, 2014, in a surprise unveiling, the backstage dressing room at the [[El Capitan|El Capitan theater]] in Hollywood, CA was formally dedicated the Sherman Brothers Dressing Room. Also in 2014, he and his brother's songs and life stories were tributed in a stage musical called ''[[A Spoonful of Sherman]]''. In 2015 an [https://www.amazon.com/Spoonful-Sherman-Original-London-Recording/dp/B013BA3HTG/ref=sr_1_1?keywords=a+spoonful+of+sherman&qid=1554109157&s=gateway&sr=8-1 original cast recording] of the show was released. The show was revived several times over the next few years and it toured the [http://www.aspoonfulofsherman.com UK and Ireland in 2018].\n\nIn 2015, Sherman composed a new song \"A Kiss Goodnight\" as the exit music for the [[Disneyland Forever]] fireworks show that ran during the park's 60th anniversary celebration. The songs's title references Walt Disney's idea that the Disneyland fireworks show were \"a kiss goodnight\".\n\nSherman contributed new lyrics for the [[2016]] live-action adaptation of ''[[The Jungle Book (2016 film)|The Jungle Book]]''.\n\nDebuting at the 2017 [[D23 Expo]], Disney Editions published the picture book: ''A Kiss Goodnight'' (ISBN 978-1484782286) written by Sherman and illustrated by [[Floyd Norman]].<ref>https://www.laughingplace.com/w/articles/2017/07/15/d23-expo-book-review-kiss-goodnight/</ref>\n\nIn 2018, Sherman marked his 90th birthday with a star-studded retrospective celebration of the Sherman Brothers songbook at the Academy of Motion Pictures, Arts and Sciences in Beverly Hills, CA.<ref>http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/movies/la-et-mn-sherman-brothers-academy-20180613-story.html</ref>\n\nSherman contributed three new songs for the live-action [[Winnie The Pooh]] film: ''[[Christopher_Robin_(film)|Christopher Robin]]'', and performs \"''Busy Doing Nothing''\" in an end-credits scene. On July 30, 2018, Soundstage A at the [[Walt Disney Studios ]] in Burbank, CA, where many of the [[Sherman Brothers]] songs were recorded, was officially renamed the [[Sherman Brothers Stage]].<ref>https://wdwnt.com/2018/07/walt-disney-studios-soundstage-a-dedicated-to-sherman-brothers/</ref>\n\n==Gallery==\n<gallery position=\"center\" captionalign=\"center\">\nRichardmsherman and walter.jpg|Richard M.
