The rhyme has been altered over the centuries, but the most popular one to this day is as follows:\n\n<poem>\nHumpty Dumpty sat on a wall\nHumpty Dumpty had a great fall\nAll the king's horses and all the king's men\nCouldn't put Humpty together again\n</poem>\n\n==Appearances==\n===Animation===\n====''[[Mother Goose Melodies]]''====\nHumpty Dumpty appears on one of the pages of Mother Goose's magic storybook, standing on his wall and laughing. It eventually unnerves Mother Goose's pet goose to the point that it smacks Humpty Dumpty from its wall, causing him to shatter.\n====''[[Old King Cole]]''====\nIn this sequel of sorts to ''Mother Goose Melodies'', Humpty Dumpty appears among the numerous nursery rhyme characters ruled by Old King Cole.\n\n===Live-action===\n====''[[Alice Through the Looking Glass]]''====\nHumpty Dumpty makes a brief appearance when [[Alice Kingsleigh|Alice]] returns to Underland via Looking Glass and upon exploring, she lands at a chessboard where a black knight chess piece frightens her, who accidentally knocking Humpty off the chessboard unaware, followed by the White King sending his white chess pieces to the rescue in a plan to put Humpty back together again (alluding to the nursery rhyme) and as Alice picks up a piece of him and apologizes to him, one of the black chess pieces grabs the piece to help put him together again while [[Absolem]] (in his butterfly form) warns Alice that the [[Tarrant Hightopp|Hatter]] is in risk of dying, forcing her to go back to Underland and save his life.\n\n===Printed materials===\n====''[https://coa.inducks.org/story.php?c=W+OS++227-03 Humpty Dumpty]''====\nIn this Seven Dwarfs story that predates the ''[[Alice in Wonderland]]'' treatment of the character, Humpty Dumpty is featured. He runs to the Seven Dwarfs for help as his brother, Gumpty, has been kidnapped by [[Gustav the Giant]].\n====''Unbirthday Party with Alice in Wonderland''====\nThe concept arts of Humpty Dumpty from the feature ''Alice in Wonderland'' eventually got used in this [[1951]] comic book drawn by Al Hubbard.\n====''[https://coa.inducks.org/story.php?c=U+MMW+610 Alice's Adventures through the Looking Glass]''====\nIn this direct sequel to the 1951 film that once more incorporates many characters scrapped from the first movie, Humpty Dumpty is once more featured.\n\n==Gallery==\n<gallery position=\"center\" widths=\"185\">\nGooseygander.jpg|Humpty Dumpty as he appeared in ''Old King Cole''\n4c0341-025.jpg|Humpty Dumpty in an ''Alice in Wonderland'' comic book story\n4c0341-024.jpg\n4c0341-023.jpg\n7dwarfs49comic34.jpg\n7dwarfs49comic33.jpg\n7dwarfs49comic31.jpg\n7dwarfs49comic30.jpg\n7dwarfs49comic29.jpg\nEnhanced-10811-1459344112-5.png\nHumptyhowie.jpg\n</gallery>\n\n==Trivia==\n*John Sessions was originally set to voice Humpty Dumpty, but was later replaced by [[Wally Wingert]] for unknown reasons.\n*Interestingly, the original nursery rhyme does not actually state that Humpty Dumpty is an egg with some art depictions having him as a portly child.\n\n==See also==\n*[[Humpty|Humpty Dumpty]] from [[Goldie & Bear]]\n\n{{DEFAULTSORT:Dumpty, Humpty}}\n\n{{Alice in Wonderland}}\n{{Disney Crossy Road}}\n[[Category:Singing characters]]\n[[Category:Males]]\n[[Category:Anthropomorphic characters]]\n[[Category:Silly Symphonies characters]]\n[[Category:Alice in Wonderland characters]]\n[[Category:Comic characters]]\n[[Category:Characters]]\n[[Category:Disney characters]]\n[[Category:Animated characters]]\n[[Category:Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs characters]]\n[[Category:Neutral characters]]\n[[Category:Tim Burton's Alice in Wonderland characters]]\n[[Category:Disney Crossy Road characters]]\n[[Category:Characters in video games]]\n[[Category:Live-action characters]]"
