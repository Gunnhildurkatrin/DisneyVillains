"{{Infobox_character|rides = [[Walt Disney's Enchanted Tiki Room]]<br>\n[[Disney's Polynesian Village Resort]]|designer = [[Rolly Crump]]|voice = [[Ginny Tyler]]|image = Pele.jpg|inspiration = Pele from M\u0101ori mythology|occupation=Goddess of Fire and Volcanoes|family=The Tiki Gods}}{{Quote|I'm the one who's really sad, when I smile it comes out mad!|Pele}}\n\n'''Pele''', the Goddess of Fire and Volcanoes, is one of the Tiki Goddesses in the pre-show of [[Walt Disney's Enchanted Tiki Room]].\n\n== History ==\n\n=== Background ===\nPele is the short-tempered goddess of fire and volcanoes, in traditional M\u0101ori myth she is also affiliated as the mother goddess of the Pacific islands.  Some cultural practices include making offers to Pele at the Halema\u02bbuma\u02bbu crater\u00a0with flowers, food and gin.  Some regions even have Pele as the feature of a sort of urban-legend where she appears on the roads of the Pali highway at night to stop cars and trucks with her powers and can only be passed by feeding pork to her dog.\n\n== Appearances ==\n\n=== Walt Disney's Enchanted Tiki Room ===\nPele's idol appears in the garden pre-show of the Enchanted Tiki Room in [[Disneyland]].  She informs guests on her presence in Polynesian myth and has fire blow from her head.<poem>\n''Jealous Pele's Angry Scorn''\n''Is Known to Every Native Born''\n''With Mighty blast or Simple Cough''\n''She Blows Her Bloomin' Top Right Off!''\n</poem>\n\n=== [[Disney's Polynesian Village Resort]] ===\nHer village appears in Disney's Polynesian Village Resort, built into the landscaping.\n\n=== [[Tahitian Terrace]] ===\n[[Hong Kong Disneyland]]'s Tahitian Terrace features an idol of Pele along with other Tiki Room statues.\n\n=== [[Trader Sam's]] ===\nPele, [[Ngendei]], [[Koro]] and [[Rongo]] all decorate the bar-taps in these bars and have glowing eyes.  In Trader Sam's Grog Grotto, one of the bottles behind the bar reads, \"Pele's potion\".\n\n==Other appearances==\n\n=== Television Appearances ===\n\n==== ''[[It's a Small World: The Animated Series]]'' ====\nShe makes a cameo appearance in Episode 5 of ''[[It's a Small World: The Animated Series]]''.\u00a0\n\n=== Printed Materials ===\n\n==== [[Disney Kingdoms]] ====\nIn [[Disney Kingdoms: Enchanted Tiki Room]]\u00a0she and the other Tiki Gods have small roles as animate statues roaming the island. In the event that an animal wishes to lose the gift of speech, Pele and the others take part in a ceremony to take it back, though this is a rare occurrence.\n\n==== [[Tales from Adventureland]] ====\nIn ''[[Tales from Adventureland: The Keymaster's Quest]]'', Pele, and the other Tiki Gods are a man-made security system to protect the rare birds of the otherwise magical Tiki Room by frightening superstitious poachers, with the fiery effect of Pele's statue being far more intimidating.\n\n== Trivia ==\n* The fictional goddess [[Te Fiti]] in [[Moana (film)|Moana]] was likely inspired by Pele.\n\n==Gallery==\n<gallery>\nIASW Pele.png|Pele in ''[[It's a Small World: The Animated Series]]''.\nTiki Room Disney Kingdoms 01.jpg|Pele on the cover of [[Disney Kingdoms: Enchanted Tiki Room]]\npele-enchanted-tiki-room.jpg\n</gallery>{{Enchanted Tiki Room}}\n{{Disney Kingdoms}}\n[[Category:Characters]]\n[[Category:Character stubs]]\n[[Category:Disney characters]]\n[[Category:Females]]\n[[Category:Objects]]\n[[Category:Magic Users]]\n[[Category:Theme park characters]]\n[[Category:Enchanted Tiki Room characters]]\n[[Category:Goddesses]]\n[[Category:Polynesian Mythology]]\n[[Category:Disney Kingdoms characters]]\n[[Category:Animated characters]]"
